# What I do not want (A saint said in Corinth)

> A saint said in Corinth, ``I do
>     What I do not want.'' Inspired 
>     Word! Most assuredly true, 
>     If one judges Jews by my tired 
>     Heart. I do live out this Pauline 
>     Part against my will.  The tingle 
>     Inside (Is it Sin, or Caffeine?)
>     Taunts me.  It whispers a single
>     Dreaded phrase relentlessly, 
>     A quietness unheard. ``You err,''
>     It says, ``infinitesimally.''
>     And then it touches me, I dare
>     Say like my mother when she said 
>     She loved me, as I lay in bed.
>
>     I did not believe.  She needed
>     Me too much.  It was obvious.
>     ``Do not touch!  You must not love us,''
>     Said my Selves, which succeeded 
>     Only in attracting her.
>     ``You are a wonderful boy, really.
>     My pride and joy. Son, are you sure
>     You are okay? Silly
>     Arguments like the most recent
>     Are natural in a family.
>     Your adolescence is hard on me.
>     Please understand, it is decent
>     (I believe) to fight openly
>     And share our feelings honestly.''
>
>     I am revolted as her fat
>     Arms rub my back, my hair ... bottom?
>     ``Does this happen elsewhere often?
>     Do other mothers,'' I think, ``pat
>     Sons similarly disgusted?
>     How come I do not love her?
>     Does she want me as my father?''
>     Nothing ever happened.  Must it?
>     Enough occurred so I cover
>     My contempt for care to this day.
>     It touches me.  I pull away 
>     From the comfort of another
>     Who loves me, but not the one
>     (The prodigal) I could become.

*Edited from 3/4/88*
